About

I'm a software engineer based in Madison, Wisconsin, specializing in language technology. With a Master's degree in Human Language Technology from the University of Arizona, I've spent the past seven years building search and conversational AI products across legal tech, healthcare, and finance sectors.

My work sits at the intersection of natural language processing, data analysis, and creating intuitive user experiences. I'm particularly interested in making complex concepts accessible to non-experts through metaphor, clear visualizations, and easily comprehensible analysis.

When I'm not coding, you'll find me flying virtual fighter jets in VR, photographing the world, or climbing at the local gym or, very occasionally, the crag.

Projects

The Fraud Behind Election Fraud

An interactive statistical analysis debunking claims of electronic vote manipulation in the 2024 US election. Using Clark County, Nevada data, this project demonstrates how misunderstandings of basic statistics, can lead to false conclusions about election integrity, especially when presented in a deceptive manner.

Features interactive visualizations, statistical simulations, and a thorough breakdown of methodological errors in conspiracy theories.
